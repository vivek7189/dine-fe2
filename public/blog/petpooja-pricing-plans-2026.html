1. What Is Petpooja?

Petpooja is one of India's most widely used restaurant POS and billing software platforms. Founded in 2011 and headquartered in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, Petpooja has built its reputation as a full-featured restaurant management system serving over 75,000 restaurants across India — from small standalone dhabas to mid-size restaurant chains.

The platform covers the core workflow of a restaurant operation: billing and KOT (Kitchen Order Ticket), inventory management, table management, basic CRM, sales reporting, and integration with delivery platforms like Zomato and Swiggy. For many restaurant owners in the 2015–2022 era, Petpooja was one of the few credible options available. The restaurant POS market in India was small, and Petpooja was early enough to build significant trust and market share.

In 2026, the landscape has shifted significantly. More modern, AI-powered, and dramatically cheaper alternatives now exist. Restaurant owners who are comparison shopping are right to ask: is Petpooja still worth the cost it commands? And more importantly — what does it actually cost?

Petpooja's Core Claim

Petpooja positions itself as a comprehensive, enterprise-grade restaurant management platform suitable for all types of food businesses — QSRs, fine dining, cloud kitchens, cafes, bakeries, and multi-outlet chains. It claims to offer deep integrations with the Indian restaurant ecosystem including Zomato, Swiggy, ONDC, and various payment gateways.

Important Disclaimer on Pricing

  • Actual pricing varies significantly based on your location, outlet type, number of users, negotiation with resellers, and plan selected.

3. Hidden Costs Most Petpooja Users Don't Expect

The monthly subscription number is just the starting point. Based on feedback from restaurant owners who use or have used Petpooja, here are the costs that consistently catch people off guard.

1. GST on Subscription (18%)

Every software subscription in India attracts 18% GST. This is not a Petpooja-specific issue, but it is something many restaurant owners fail to factor in during the evaluation stage. If Petpooja quotes you ₹5,000/month, your actual billing is ₹5,900/month. Over a year, that is an extra ₹10,800 in GST on top of your ₹60,000 annual subscription. Always confirm whether pricing is quoted inclusive or exclusive of GST.

2. Add-On Module Charges

Petpooja's core plan often does not include all features. Many features that you might expect to be standard are sold as paid add-ons. Common add-ons and their estimated costs:

  • Loyalty and CRM module: ₹1,000 – 2,500/month extra
  • Online ordering module: ₹1,500 – 3,000/month extra
  • Advanced inventory module: Sometimes requires plan upgrade
  • WhatsApp integration: ₹500 – 1,500/month extra
  • Kitchen Display System (KDS): May require additional hardware + software fee
  • Multi-branch reporting dashboard: Premium plan or add-on required

It is common for a restaurant owner to sign up for a mid-tier plan and then discover that the specific features they wanted — loyalty program, WhatsApp integration, advanced inventory — are add-ons. The bill climbs by ₹3,000–7,000/month before they realise it.

3. Zomato and Swiggy Integration Fees

Integrating Petpooja with Zomato and Swiggy is often not seamless or free. Some users report one-time integration setup fees of ₹2,000–5,000 per platform. Others report ongoing monthly fees for maintaining the integration. Even if the software integration itself is included, the process of getting it configured, tested, and working correctly often requires paid technician time.

4. Hardware Maintenance

POS hardware — printers, terminals, cash drawers — breaks. Thermal printer heads wear out. Tablets need screen replacements. Power fluctuations damage components. Annual hardware maintenance contracts typically cost ₹3,000–8,000/year. Unplanned repairs can cost significantly more. This ongoing cost is absent when you use a software POS that runs on your own devices.

5. Training Charges

Initial training is often included in the setup fee, but ongoing training is not always free. When you hire new staff, onboarding them on Petpooja may require formal training sessions, especially if your team is not tech-savvy. Some resellers charge ₹1,000–3,000 for additional training visits. This cost is harder to quantify but adds up over the lifecycle of your subscription.

6. Data Migration When You Switch

This is the hidden cost that only matters when you eventually decide to leave — but it matters a lot. When you accumulate years of transaction history, customer data, inventory records, and menu data in Petpooja's system, extracting and migrating that data to a new platform is not always straightforward or free. Some users report spending ₹5,000–20,000 on data migration services when switching away from Petpooja.

7. Annual Price Increases

Software subscriptions in India, like everywhere else, tend to increase at renewal. Multiple Petpooja users have reported 10–20% price increases on annual renewal. Over a 3-year period, this compounds: a ₹5,000/month subscription in Year 1 becomes ₹5,500 in Year 2 and ₹6,050 in Year 3. Budget for this in your long-term cost calculations.

Full Hidden Cost Checklist Before Signing Petpooja

  • Ask: Is the quoted price inclusive or exclusive of 18% GST?
  • Ask: Which features I want are included vs. charged as add-ons?
  • Ask: Is Zomato/Swiggy integration included or an additional charge?
  • Ask: What is the annual maintenance charge for hardware?
  • Ask: Are additional training sessions free or charged?
  • Ask: What happens to my data if I cancel? Can I export it? Is there a fee?
  • Ask: Is pricing locked or does it increase at renewal? By how much?
  • Ask: What is the cancellation policy and notice period?

5. Petpooja Pros and Cons: An Honest Assessment

Petpooja has been around for over a decade and has built genuine strengths. It also has real weaknesses. Here is an honest assessment based on available user feedback and market observations — not marketing copy.

Petpooja Pros

  • Established brand: 15 years in the Indian market, trusted by 75,000+ restaurants
  • Strong Zomato/Swiggy integration: Order aggregation from delivery platforms is generally reliable
  • Large support team: Dedicated support staff available in multiple Indian languages
  • Offline mode: Works without internet, critical for restaurants with unreliable connectivity
  • Hardware ecosystem: Established supply chain for printers, terminals, and accessories
  • GST compliance: Well-tested GST billing for Indian tax requirements
  • Reseller network: Local technicians available for setup and maintenance in most cities

Petpooja Cons

  • Expensive for small restaurants: ₹3,000–8,000/month plus GST is heavy for a single-outlet restaurant
  • Feature bloat: Complex interface with many features most small restaurants never use
  • Hardware dependency: Requires specific devices; cannot run on your existing phone or tablet
  • Add-on nickel-and-diming: Core features like loyalty and KDS often require extra monthly payment
  • No AI capabilities: No voice ordering, no AI menu extraction, no predictive inventory
  • Opaque pricing: No public pricing page; must go through sales to get a quote
  • Contract lock-in: Annual contracts make it difficult to switch without financial penalty

Who Petpooja Is Best Suited For

Petpooja makes the most sense for mid-to-large restaurant operations — chains with 5+ outlets, restaurants with high daily transaction volumes, or businesses that have historically relied on Petpooja and have teams trained on the system. For these use cases, the established infrastructure, reseller network, and robust offline mode provide real value.

For single-outlet restaurants, small cafes, new restaurants, cloud kitchens, and budget-conscious owners, the total cost of ownership is difficult to justify when modern alternatives offer equivalent or superior features at a fraction of the cost.
