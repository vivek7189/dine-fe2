FSSAI License for Restaurants: Complete Registration Guide (2026)

By DineOpen Team • March 10, 2026 • 14 min read
Every restaurant, cloud kitchen, and food business in India must have a valid FSSAI license to operate legally. The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) regulates all food businesses under the FSS Act, 2006. Operating without a license can attract fines up to Rs 5 lakh and imprisonment of up to 6 months. This guide walks you through the entire FSSAI registration process for restaurants -- from choosing the right license type to completing your application on the FoSCoS portal.

1. What is FSSAI and Why Restaurants Need It

The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) is an autonomous statutory body established under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006. It operates under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India. FSSAI is responsible for setting standards for food products, regulating their manufacture, storage, distribution, and sale, and ensuring the availability of safe and wholesome food for the people of India.

For restaurant owners, FSSAI licensing is not optional -- it is a legal requirement. Every food business operator (FBO) in India, whether you run a fine-dining restaurant, a small dhaba, a cloud kitchen, a food truck, or a catering service, must obtain an FSSAI license or registration before commencing operations. This applies equally to dine-in restaurants, takeaway outlets, and businesses that sell food through platforms like Zomato and Swiggy.

Penalties for Operating Without FSSAI License

  • Fine: Up to Rs 5,00,000 for operating without a valid license
  • Imprisonment: Up to 6 months for repeat or severe violations
  • Closure Order: Food safety officers can order immediate shutdown of the premises
  • Product Seizure: All food products can be confiscated and destroyed
  • Platform Delisting: Zomato, Swiggy, and other aggregators require valid FSSAI numbers -- you cannot list without one

Beyond legal compliance, an FSSAI license builds trust with your customers. The 14-digit FSSAI license number displayed on your restaurant signage, menu cards, food packaging, and bills signals that your establishment meets government-mandated food safety standards. In an era where customers are increasingly health-conscious and aware of food safety, this credibility is invaluable for your restaurant's reputation.

The FSSAI license also covers hygiene standards for your premises, water quality requirements, food handling practices, and storage conditions. During inspections, FSSAI officers check for cleanliness of the kitchen, proper food storage temperatures, pest control measures, personal hygiene of staff, and waste disposal methods. Meeting these standards is not just about passing an inspection -- it is about genuinely serving safe food to your customers.

2. Types of FSSAI Licenses for Restaurants

FSSAI offers three types of licenses based on the scale and turnover of your food business. Choosing the correct category is critical -- applying for the wrong type is one of the most common reasons for application rejection. Here is a detailed breakdown of each type.

Basic FSSAI Registration

Basic Registration is designed for small food businesses, including small restaurants, dhabas, food stalls, home-based food businesses, and petty food operators. This is the simplest form of FSSAI compliance and is sufficient for most new and small restaurant ventures.

  • Eligibility: Annual turnover below Rs 12 lakh
  • Fee: Rs 100 per year
  • Validity: 1 to 5 years (you choose at the time of application)
  • Form Required: Form-A
  • Inspection: Not mandatory, but may be conducted randomly
  • Processing Time: 7 working days (typically)

State FSSAI License

The State License is required for medium-sized food businesses operating within a single state. Most established restaurants, mid-size restaurant chains operating in one state, and catering businesses fall under this category.

  • Eligibility: Annual turnover between Rs 12 lakh and Rs 20 crore
  • Fee: Rs 2,000 to Rs 5,000 per year (based on turnover slab)
  • Validity: 1 to 5 years
  • Form Required: Form-B
  • Inspection: Mandatory before license issuance
  • Processing Time: 30 to 60 days

Central FSSAI License

The Central License is for large-scale food businesses with operations spanning multiple states, or those involved in import/export of food products. Large restaurant chains, hotel groups, and food manufacturing units typically need this license.

  • Eligibility: Annual turnover above Rs 20 crore, or operations in 2+ states
  • Fee: Rs 7,500 per year
  • Validity: 1 to 5 years
  • Form Required: Form-B
  • Inspection: Mandatory and more thorough
  • Processing Time: 30 to 60 days

Which License Does Your Restaurant Need?

  • Small dhaba, food stall, home kitchen: Basic Registration (Rs 100/year)
  • Single restaurant, cafe, cloud kitchen (turnover Rs 12L-20Cr): State License (Rs 2,000-5,000/year)
  • Restaurant chain across states (turnover Rs 20Cr+): Central License (Rs 7,500/year)
  • New restaurant (unsure of turnover): Start with Basic Registration, upgrade later as turnover grows

An important point: if your restaurant's turnover crosses the threshold for your current license category, you must upgrade to the appropriate license within 30 days. FSSAI conducts random audits, and operating with the wrong license category is treated as a violation. 3. Documents Required for FSSAI Registration

Having all your documents ready before starting the application process is essential. Incomplete documentation is the number one reason for delays and rejections. The documents required vary slightly between Basic Registration and State/Central License, but here is the comprehensive checklist for restaurant owners.

For Basic Registration (Form-A)

  • Identity Proof of the Owner: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, or Passport
  • Passport-size Photograph: Recent photograph of the proprietor or authorised signatory
  • Address Proof of Business Premises: Rent agreement, electricity bill, property tax receipt, or ownership deed
  • List of Food Products: Complete list of food items you plan to manufacture, serve, or sell

For State and Central License (Form-B)

In addition to all the documents listed above for Basic Registration, you will need the following.

  • Food Safety Management Plan: A document outlining your food handling, storage, and safety procedures. This includes how you maintain hygiene, control temperatures, prevent cross-contamination, and handle food waste.
  • Layout Plan of the Premises: A blueprint or floor plan showing the kitchen layout, storage areas, dining area, washrooms, and waste disposal zone. The plan should demonstrate adequate ventilation, lighting, and separation between raw and cooked food areas.
  • NOC from Municipality / Local Authority: A No Objection Certificate from your municipal corporation or panchayat confirming that your premises are approved for running a food business.
  • Water Test Report: A laboratory test report confirming that the water used in your restaurant meets potable water standards. Get this done from a NABL-accredited lab. The report is typically valid for 6 months.
  • List of Equipment and Machinery: A complete inventory of all kitchen equipment, refrigeration units, cooking appliances, and food processing machinery used in your restaurant.
  • Partnership Deed / MOA / AOA: If your restaurant is a partnership firm, LLP, or company, provide the relevant incorporation documents.
  • IEC Code: Only required if you import food products or ingredients. Issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT).

Pro Tip: Document Preparation Checklist

  • All documents must be clear, legible scans -- blurry or cropped uploads are rejected
  • File format: PDF or JPEG, each file under 2 MB
  • Rent agreement must be notarised or registered
  • Water test report should be less than 6 months old
  • Food safety plan -- use FSSAI's template available on foscos.fssai.gov.in

4. Step-by-Step FSSAI Registration Process

FSSAI has moved its entire registration and licensing process online through the Food Safety and Compliance System (FoSCoS) portal at foscos.fssai.gov.in. Here is the complete step-by-step process to get your restaurant's FSSAI license.

Step 1: Create an Account on FoSCoS Portal

Visit foscos.fssai.gov.in and click on "Apply for License/Registration." You will need to create a new account using your mobile number and email address. Verify your account through the OTP sent to your mobile. Once verified, you can log in and access the application dashboard.

Step 2: Select the Correct License Type

Based on your restaurant's annual turnover, select the appropriate category -- Basic Registration, State License, or Central License. The portal will guide you based on your inputs, but make sure you understand the turnover thresholds discussed in Section 2 to avoid choosing the wrong category.

Step 3: Fill in the Application Form

For Basic Registration, fill out Form-A. For State or Central License, fill out Form-B. The form asks for details about your business entity (proprietorship, partnership, company), premises address, type of food business activity (manufacturing, serving, catering, etc.), list of food products, and details of the food safety management system in place. Fill every field carefully -- errors or omissions lead to queries and delays.

Step 4: Upload Required Documents

Upload all the documents listed in Section 3 in the prescribed format. Ensure each file is clear, properly named, and within the size limit. Double-check that the rent agreement address matches the address filled in the form -- mismatches are a common cause for rejection.

Step 5: Pay the Application Fee

Pay the applicable fee online through net banking, debit card, credit card, or UPI. The fee depends on your license type and the validity period you choose (1 to 5 years). Paying for multiple years upfront saves you from annual renewal hassles.

FSSAI License Fees at a Glance

  • Basic Registration: Rs 100/year (e.g., Rs 500 for 5 years)
  • State License (Rs 12L-20Cr turnover): Rs 2,000 - Rs 5,000/year
  • Central License (Rs 20Cr+ turnover): Rs 7,500/year
  • Late Renewal Penalty: Rs 100 per day of delay

Step 6: Application Review and Inspection

After submission, your application is assigned to the relevant Food Safety Officer (FSO). For Basic Registration, the application is usually auto-approved or reviewed within 7 working days. For State and Central Licenses, the FSO will schedule an inspection of your restaurant premises. During the inspection, the officer checks kitchen hygiene, food storage conditions, water quality, pest control, waste disposal, and staff hygiene practices. Cooperate fully and ensure your premises are in top condition on the inspection day.

Step 7: License Issued

If everything is in order, your FSSAI license is generated digitally and made available on the FoSCoS portal for download. You will receive a 14-digit FSSAI license number that must be prominently displayed at your restaurant and printed on all food packaging, menu cards, and bills. The entire process takes 7 days for Basic Registration and 30 to 60 days for State and Central Licenses.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid During FSSAI Registration

Many restaurant owners face unnecessary delays, rejections, or penalties due to avoidable mistakes during the FSSAI registration process. Here are the most common pitfalls and how to steer clear of them.

Mistake 1: Selecting the Wrong License Category

This is the most frequent error. Restaurant owners sometimes apply for Basic Registration even when their turnover exceeds Rs 12 lakh, thinking it will save money on fees. FSSAI audits can catch this, and operating under the wrong category is treated as operating without a valid license -- attracting the same penalties. If you are unsure, apply for the State License to be safe. You can always apply for a lower category later if your turnover qualifies.

Mistake 2: Submitting Incomplete or Incorrect Documents

Uploading blurry scans, missing documents, or documents with mismatched addresses is the top reason for application queries and delays. Before submitting, cross-verify that every document is legible, up to date, and that the business name and address are consistent across all documents. Have someone else review your application before final submission.

Mistake 3: Not Renewing the License on Time

Your FSSAI license has a fixed validity period (1 to 5 years). You must apply for renewal at least 30 days before the expiry date. Late renewal attracts a penalty of Rs 100 per day. If you let the license lapse completely and continue operating, you face the full non-compliance penalties -- fines up to Rs 5 lakh. Set a calendar reminder 60 days before expiry to begin the renewal process.

Mistake 4: Not Displaying the FSSAI License Number

Once you receive your license, you are legally required to display the 14-digit FSSAI license number prominently at your restaurant premises. It must also appear on all food packaging, menu cards, bills/invoices, and online listings (Zomato, Swiggy, Google Business). Failure to display the number can result in penalties during inspections and even suspension of your license.

Mistake 5: Ignoring Food Safety Standards Post-Licensing

Getting the license is not the end -- it is the beginning. FSSAI conducts periodic surprise inspections. Failing to maintain hygiene standards, not keeping food safety records, using expired ingredients, or not following proper food storage practices can lead to license suspension or cancellation. Treat food safety as an ongoing daily responsibility, not a one-time compliance task.

FSSAI Renewal Timeline

  • 60 days before expiry: Start gathering updated documents (water test report, etc.)
  • 30 days before expiry: Submit renewal application on FoSCoS portal
  • On expiry date: If renewal is pending, you may continue operating until a decision is made
  • After expiry (no renewal filed): Rs 100/day late fee + risk of closure order
